Do I Need an Electrician to Install an EV Charger?

For Level 2 installation in Tulsa, yes. Here's why it's not just a recommendation, and what you give up by skipping it.

Last verified: June 2026
💡
A licensed electrician is required to pull the City of Tulsa permit for Level 2 EV charger installation. Without that permit, you lose the PSO $200 rebate and can run into insurance and resale problems down the road.

For a Level 2 EV charger in Tulsa, you need a licensed electrician. Not because the work is necessarily complicated, a straightforward garage install is a half-day job, but because Oklahoma requires a permit, and that permit requires licensed work. The City of Tulsa won't approve unpermitted electrical installations, and PSO won't pay the $200 rebate without proof of permitted installation.

What the Law Actually Says

Oklahoma state law requires an electrical permit for any new 240V circuit. The permit must be pulled before work begins. In Tulsa, permits are issued through the City of Tulsa Development Services department. Licensed electricians can pull permits on your behalf. Homeowners can sometimes pull owner-builder permits for their own primary residence, but it's a slower process and requires you to be the one doing the inspection — which most city inspectors approach with skepticism when the work involves EV charging equipment.

The practical reality: hire a licensed electrician. The permit process is then standard and fast.

Why the PSO Rebate Makes It Non-Negotiable

PSO's $200 rebate for qualifying EV charger installation requires a properly permitted installation. If you do the work yourself without a permit, you're leaving $200 on the table. Add in the cost difference between a DIY electrical project and a professional one, and the math usually isn't in favor of DIY anyway.

The rebate application asks for proof of purchase and installation details. PSO cross-references this against permit records in some cases. Don't risk the rebate over a few hours of DIY work.

What a Licensed Electrician Actually Does

The scope of work for a typical Tulsa garage installation:

  • 1

    Panel assessment

    Before anything else, the electrician opens your panel to check available capacity and whether you need a new breaker slot or a full panel upgrade. Most newer Tulsa homes are fine. Older homes in Midtown or South Tulsa with 100-amp service may need an upgrade.

  • 2

    Permit pulled

    Your electrician files for the electrical permit with the City of Tulsa before work starts. This is non-negotiable and happens before any drilling or wiring.

  • 3

    Circuit run and charger installed

    A dedicated 240V, 50-amp circuit runs from your panel to the garage. The charger is mounted at the right height and location for your parking setup. This is usually the bulk of the work, 2 to 4 hours for an attached garage.

  • 4

    Inspection scheduled

    After installation, your electrician contacts the City of Tulsa to schedule an inspection. The inspector verifies the work meets code. This usually happens within a few business days of the install.

What Happens If You Skip the Electrician

People sometimes skip the electrician thinking they'll save a few hundred dollars. Here's what that actually risks:

  • PSO rebate forfeited: $200 gone.
  • Homeowner's insurance: Most policies exclude coverage for damage caused by unpermitted electrical work. A charger fire, however unlikely, would be your problem.
  • Home resale: Buyers' inspectors find unpermitted work. You'll either fix it at the buyer's demand or disclose it and take a hit on price.
  • Safety: A 50-amp circuit done wrong is a serious fire hazard. This isn't fearmongering. Undersized wire gauges and improper connections on 240V circuits do cause fires.

How to Find a Good Electrician in Tulsa

Look for licensed electricians with experience in EV charger installations specifically. Ask whether they've pulled EV charger permits with the City of Tulsa before — the answer tells you quickly whether they know what they're doing. Get at least two quotes. Prices vary, and there's no single right answer.
